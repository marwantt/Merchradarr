# How To Read Amazon Bsr For Merch

In the world of Amazon Merch by Amazon, understanding how to effectively use **Best Sellers Rank (BSR)** is crucial for validating and choosing profitable niches. This tutorial will guide you through what BSR is, how to find it, interpret its numbers, and strategically use it to gauge competition and validate your niche in under 15 minutes.

## What is BSR?

**Best Sellers Rank (BSR)** is a metric provided by Amazon that indicates how well a product is selling relative to other products in the same category. Each product on Amazon is assigned a BSR, which is updated hourly based on recent and historical sales. A lower BSR indicates higher sales volume, whereas a higher BSR suggests fewer sales.

## How to Find BSR on a Product Page

Finding the BSR on a product page is straightforward:

1. Navigate to the product page on Amazon.
2. Scroll down to the "Product Details" section.
3. Look for the "Best Sellers Rank" in the list of details. It will typically be shown as a number followed by the category.

For example:
```
Best Sellers Rank: #1,234 in Clothing, Shoes & Jewelry
```

## Understanding BSR Numbers and Sales Volume

The BSR number provides an indirect way to estimate a product's sales volume. While BSR fluctuates, it can give you an idea of how well a product is performing. Here's a rough reference table for the Clothing category:

| **BSR Range**         | **Estimated Sales Per Month** |
|-----------------------|-------------------------------|
| 1 - 1,000             | 1,000+                        |
| 1,001 - 5,000         | 500 - 1,000                   |
| 5,001 - 10,000        | 200 - 500                     |
| 10,001 - 50,000       | 50 - 200                      |
| 50,001 - 100,000      | 10 - 50                       |
| 100,001 - 500,000     | 1 - 10                        |
| 500,001 and above     | Less than 1                   |

**Note:** These numbers are estimates and can vary based on factors like seasonality and trends.

## Using BSR to Gauge Competition

BSR not only helps in understanding sales volume but also in gauging competition. Here's how:

- **Low BSR (1-10,000):** Indicates high sales volume; likely high competition.
- **Moderate BSR (10,001-50,000):** Moderate sales volume; potentially less competition.
- **High BSR (50,001 and above):** Lower sales volume; likely less competition.

## Targeting BSR as a New Seller

As a new seller, targeting products with a BSR between 50,000 and 100,000 in the Clothing category is often a good strategy. This range balances moderate sales volume with manageable competition, providing an opportunity to enter the market and gain traction without being overwhelmed by established sellers.

## Step-by-Step Process to Validate Your Niche Using BSR

Follow these steps to validate your Merch by Amazon niche using BSR research efficiently:

### Step 1: Brainstorm Niche Ideas

- Start by listing potential niches based on trends, interests, or hobbies.
- Use tools like Google Trends or social media platforms to check the popularity of your niche ideas.

### Step 2: Search on Amazon

- Enter your niche keywords in the Amazon search bar.
- Filter results by category to focus specifically on "Clothing, Shoes & Jewelry."

### Step 3: Analyze Product Listings

- Open the top 5-10 product listings relevant to your niche.
- Scroll to the "Product Details" section to note the BSR of each product.

### Step 4: Evaluate BSR Data

- Compare the BSRs to the reference table provided.
- Look for patterns: Are the top products consistently within the 10,000 to 50,000 BSR range, suggesting moderate competition?

### Step 5: Assess Competition

- Check the number of reviews and ratings. High reviews often indicate strong competition.
- Consider the design quality and uniqueness of top-selling products.

### Step 6: Make a Decision

- If the BSR indicates moderate sales and the competition is not overly saturated, the niche is worth exploring.
- If not, consider refining your niche with more specific keywords or ideas.

### Step 7: Validate Quickly

- Use a spreadsheet to track your findings, noting BSRs, product names, and any other relevant data.
- Compile this data to make informed decisions about which niches to pursue.
